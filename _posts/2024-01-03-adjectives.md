## Los Adjetivos

Adjectives in Spanish must agree in gender and number with the nouns they modify.

### Gender Agreement

**Masculine:**
- El libro **rojo** (the red book)
- El gato **pequeño** (the small cat)

**Feminine:**
- La casa **roja** (the red house)
- La gata **pequeña** (the small cat)

### Number Agreement

**Singular:**
- El niño **alto** (the tall boy)
- La niña **alta** (the tall girl)

**Plural:**
- Los niños **altos** (the tall boys)
- Las niñas **altas** (the tall girls)

### Common Adjectives

**Ending in -o/-a:**
- Bonito/Bonita (pretty)
- Feo/Fea (ugly)
- Alto/Alta (tall)
- Bajo/Baja (short)
- Nuevo/Nueva (new)
- Viejo/Vieja (old)

**Ending in -e (same for both genders):**
- Grande (big)
- Inteligente (intelligent)
- Interesante (interesting)
- Fuerte (strong)

**Ending in consonant (same for both genders):**
- Fácil (easy)
- Difícil (difficult)
- Azul (blue)
- Gris (gray)

### Position of Adjectives

Most adjectives come **after** the noun:
- Un coche **rápido** (a fast car)
- Una película **interesante** (an interesting movie)

Some adjectives come **before** the noun:
- Un **buen** libro (a good book)
- Una **mala** idea (a bad idea)
- **Mucho** dinero (much money)

### Examples

- Los **perros negros** (the black dogs)
- Las **casas grandes** (the big houses)
- Un **hombre inteligente** (an intelligent man)
- Una **mujer fuerte** (a strong woman)
